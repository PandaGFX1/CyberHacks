Types of Connections 

- Cables Connections 
    
    - Ethernet (Twisted Pair and Coaxial Standards) (802.3) 
        
    - Fiber Optic 
        
- Wireless Connections 
    
    - WiFi (802.11) 
        

Copper Cabling – Use electrical signals to pass data between networks 

- Coaxial – Almost obsolete for new LAN installations – Cable TV and residential internet still use coaxial 
    
    - Reduces induction, electromagnetic interference (EMI) and radio frequence (RF interferance 
        
- Twisted-Pair Cabling – Consists of MULTIPLE copper cables twisted into pairs. Reduces interference from outside sources 
    
    - Unshielded Twisted Pair (UTP) - Basic twisted cable standard | Most commonly seen in home/office spaces 
        
    - Shielded Twisted Pair (STP) - Uses a foil shield to further reduce interference | Seen in modern infrastructure and static enviroments with equipment that is stationau 
        
- Twister Pair Cabling -  
    
    - Straight-Through – Used to connect two different devices (PC to switch) - Same order at both ends 
        
    - Crossover – Used to connect two similar devices (PC to PC) - Swapped at one end 
        

Fiber Optic Connections – Transmits pulses of light rather than electrical signals. It is high-bandwidth, high-speed, and can communicate across long distances. Made of a glass or plastic fiber 

Wireless Networking – Also known as WiFi or Wireless Ethernet 

- Wireless Access Point (WAP) - Antenna communicates with 
    
- Service Set Identifier (SSID) - How each network is identified – Wirless devices must be on the same SSID
