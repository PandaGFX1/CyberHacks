Baseband – Single fixed digital frequency – Occupies the entire capacity/bandwidth 

- Digital Signaling – 1's and 0's – Electrical signals 
    

Broadband – Multiple analog signals – One channel is required to send and another to receive 

- Analog Signaling – Uses continuous waveform (RF. Sound, music, etc.) 
    

Modem – MODulator/DEModulator - Networking device that converts digital signals to analog and vice versa 

Digital Telecommunications Convergence – Combination, integration, and evolution of voice, data, video, pictures, etc. 

Public Switched Telephone Network (PSTN) - World's collection of voice-oriented public telephone networks 

Plain Old Telephone Service (POTS) - Fixed-line analog telephone system (spin wheel phones) is now almost entirely digital and integraes mobile phones along with land-line 

- The internet SHARES the same infrastructure as the PSTN for intercontinental communication – Early data rates for PSTN modems were topped at 56 kbps 
    
- Costly to maintain and old - Will be completely gone by 2030, some countries are doing 2025 
    

Network Carrier Standards – OC768 is the top one 

Signal Level DS-0 (Standard PSTN phone line) - Can carry exactly one phone call or voice channel 

Digital Subscriber Line (DSL) - Modulation scheme to pack data onto copper lines or existing infrastructure (this is the PSTN). 

- Subscription Service 
    
- Offered in 3 types of service Configurations 
    

- Asymmetric DSL (ADSL) 
    
    - Residential Consumer version of DSL 
        

- Symmetric DSL (SDSL) 
    

- Very High Bitrate DSL (VDSL) 
    

Asynchronous Transfer Mode – high-speed network technology destined for use on BOTH LANs and WANs 

- Primary choice for WAN for long-haul, high-speed over PSTN 
    
- - Permanent – Dedicated connections (pre-configured by your ISP) 
        
    - Switched – Per-call basis that disconnects when the call is terminated 
        

Synchronous Optical Network (SONET) - High-speed, digital networking standard that specifies data rate over fiber-optic connections – Multiplexed, different signaling types, over fiber optic 

- Signaling rates for SONET and ATM are identical and measured in terms of OC 
    

Satellite Communications – Consists of satellites in space and Ground Stations on Earty – Communication Satellites receive, amplify, and transmit voice, music, television, telephone, and data signals 

Low Earth Orbit (LEO) 

- Most common 
    
- Smallest footprint 
    

Medium Earth Orbit (MEO) 

- Used for GPS 
    
- Moderate Footprint 
    

Highly Elliptical Orbit (HEO) 

- Elongated orbit 
    
- Requires 2+ satellites for consistent coverage 
    
- High dwell tie on a large specific footprint 
    

Geosynchronous Orbit (GSO) 

- Second most common 
    
- 24 hour orbit designed to maintain a constant footprint 
    
- 22, 236 
    
- Used for Communication and Broadcasting 
    

Geosynchronous Equatorial Orbit (GEO) 

- Specific type of GSO 
    
- Occupies a fixed location above the equator 
    

Very Small Aperture Terminal (VSAT) - Networking services such as data, voice, video, and fax transmissions are delivered using geostationary satellites. - Operate in 2 basic topologies and 3 configurations (configurations are subset of topologies) 

- Mesh Topology 
    
    - Point-to-Point 
        
- Star Topology 
    
    - Point-to-Multipoint 
        
    - Multipoint-to-Point
